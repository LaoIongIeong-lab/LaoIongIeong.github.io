"The application of categorical methods to computation has a distinguished history predating deep learning by decades. The Curry-Howard-Lambek correspondence, established through the work of Curry and Feys (1958), Howard (1980), and Lambek (1980), revealed profound connections between logic, type theory, and category theory. This correspondence underlies the categorical semantics of programming languages and laid groundwork for understanding computation through categorical lenses."
